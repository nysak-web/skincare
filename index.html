Skin Care Tips for Teenagers

Combination

For combination skin, focus on balancing the different areas. You are essentially treating two different skin types when you have combination skin. Cleansing, toning and moisturizing should be part of your daily skincare routine.

Make limited use of products with alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) and beta hydroxy acids (BHAs).

Use a gentle facial cleanser, followed by regular exfoliation and a water based moisturizer.

Use broad spectrum Sunscreen along with treating your complexion to face masks.


Product Suggestions - Balancing toner, Lightweight moisturizer

Oily

For your teenage skin you should use tinted moisturizers or BB creams instead of the foundations and products heavy on the skin. They may cause acne.

Look for ingredients like hyaluronic acid. Include one product (not both) with Salicylic acid and glycolic acid and add it into your routine slowly.

Consider adding products with Retinoids and retinols, which are Vitamin A derivatives.

Use a lightweight Sunscreen.


Product Suggestions - Oil-free moisturizer, Oil control face wash

Dry

For dry skin, use hydrating and nourishing products. Avoid harsh products. Sunscreen is important.

Limit bath or shower time to 5 to 10 minutes to avoid drying out your skin and use warm water instead of hot.

Stop using products containing alcohol, Alpha-hydoxy acid, fragrances and Retinoids.


Product Suggestions - Hydrating serum, Rich moisturizer
